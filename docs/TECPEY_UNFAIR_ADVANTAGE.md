## Part 4 — Why Users Learn Faster

### 4.1 The Science of Faster Learning

TecPey's educational design is not based on content volume — it is based on learning science. The mechanisms that produce faster learning:

**Active recall over passive reading.**
Students retrieve information through knowledge checks, not just read it. Active recall produces 50–80% better retention than re-reading (based on Roediger & Butler, 2011).

**Spaced repetition.**
Key concepts are reviewed at expanding intervals. Spaced repetition produces 2–3x better long-term retention than massed study (Cepeda et al., 2006).

**Mastery gating.**
Students cannot advance until they demonstrate competence. This prevents the "I kind of understand" zone where most education fails.

**Interleaving.**
Practice mixes concepts from multiple modules. This is harder in the short term but produces 40–50% better transfer (Taylor & Rohrer, 2010).

**Immediate feedback.**
Knowledge checks provide instant explanation of wrong answers. Immediate feedback closes the gap between error and correction before the error is consolidated in memory.

**Socratic AI.**
The AI Mentor asks questions instead of delivering answers. Elaborative interrogation produces deeper understanding than passive reception.

### 4.2 What TecPey Teaches That Competitors Don't

**Trading psychology.**
No Iranian platform teaches trading psychology at all. TecPey makes it a full term. The psychological dimensions of trading — FOMO, loss aversion, revenge trading, overconfidence — are the primary causes of trader failure. Teaching them directly accelerates competence.

**Behavioral self-awareness.**
The Trading DNA framework gives students a mirror. Seeing your own FOMO score and revenge trading frequency creates insight that no article can produce.

**The skill of not trading.**
When not to trade is a module in Term 4. This is knowledge that more experienced traders wish someone had taught them earlier.
