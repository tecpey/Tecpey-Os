# TecPey Academy — Curriculum Blueprint

**Phase 14 Strategic Document**
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-06-27
**Status:** Implementation-Ready

---

## Overview

TecPey Academy is structured as 7 terms (دوره) plus 3 advanced tracks. The metaphor is a school, not a course library. Students enroll, progress through structured content, and graduate.

Each term builds on the previous. Skipping terms is not permitted until an assessment demonstrates existing competency (placement test feature — Phase 2).

---

## Term Structure (Standard)

| Term | Name (FA) | Name (EN) | Focus | Duration (est.) |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | دنیای دیجیتال | Digital World | Digital literacy, blockchain basics | 3–4 weeks |
| 2 | بازار ارز دیجیتال | Crypto Markets | Market structure, assets, exchanges | 4–5 weeks |
| 3 | تحلیل بازار | Market Analysis | Technical + fundamental analysis foundations | 5–6 weeks |
| 4 | مدیریت ریسک | Risk Management | Position sizing, stop-loss, portfolio | 4–5 weeks |
| 5 | روان‌شناسی معامله‌گری | Trading Psychology | Behavioral finance, emotional discipline | 4–5 weeks |
| 6 | استراتژی و سیستم | Strategy & System | Strategy building, backtesting, system trading | 5–6 weeks |
| 7 | حرفه‌ای‌سازی | Professionalization | Career paths, compliance, continuous improvement | 3–4 weeks |

**Advanced Tracks (post-Term 7):**
- Track A: DeFi & On-Chain Analysis
- Track B: Derivatives & Futures
- Track C: Quantitative Methods

**Professional Track (post-Advanced):**
- TecPey Certified Professional (TCP)
- Mentor Certification (TecPey Certified Mentor — TCM)

## Term 1 — Digital World (دنیای دیجیتال)

### Goal
Transform a complete digital beginner into someone who understands what blockchain is, how crypto exists, why it matters, and how to stay safe online.

### Prerequisites
- None. This is the entry point.

### Skills Acquired
1. Understanding of what money is and why digital alternatives emerged
2. Basic understanding of how blockchain works (without cryptography depth)
3. Identification of different categories of cryptocurrencies
4. Ability to set up a secure account and protect it
5. Recognition of common scams and social engineering attacks
6. Ability to navigate a crypto exchange safely

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 1.1 — What is Money?
- Lessons: History of money, the problem with centralized currency, trust in monetary systems
- Exercise: Scenario — "Your bank freezes your account. What can you do?"
- Knowledge Check: 5 questions
- Format: Text + illustrated timeline

#### Module 1.2 — Blockchain Basics
- Lessons: Distributed ledger concept, blocks and chains, consensus basics, immutability
- Exercise: Interactive block-building visualization
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions
- Format: Text + interactive diagram

#### Module 1.3 — Crypto Asset Types
- Lessons: Coins vs tokens, Layer 1 vs Layer 2, stablecoins, utility vs governance, NFTs (overview only)
- Exercise: Categorization exercise — classify 10 assets by type
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 1.4 — Security First
- Lessons: Wallet types (custodial vs non-custodial), private keys, 2FA, phishing recognition, social engineering
- Exercise: Identify-the-scam exercise (5 scam scenarios, student identifies red flags)
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions
- **This module must be passed at 90% or higher — security is non-negotiable**

#### Module 1.5 — Your First Exchange Account
- Lessons: Exchange types (CEX vs DEX), KYC, deposit/withdrawal, order types (market, limit), fee structures
- Exercise: Trading Arena orientation — place first simulated order
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

### Term 1 Assessments
- **Midterm:** Modules 1.1–1.3 (25 questions, 70% pass)
- **Final Exam:** All modules (40 questions, 70% pass)
- **Simulator Requirement:** 5 completed paper trades in Trading Arena
- **Certificate:** TecPey Academy Term 1 — Digital World Foundations

## Term 2 — Crypto Markets (بازار ارز دیجیتال)

### Goal
Build a functional understanding of how crypto markets work, what drives price, and how to read basic market data responsibly.

### Prerequisites
- Term 1 certificate or placement test score ≥ 80%

### Skills Acquired
1. Reading a market depth chart and order book
2. Understanding market cycles at a conceptual level
3. Ability to research a crypto project with structured framework
4. Understanding of liquidity and volatility
5. Safe use of portfolio tracking tools

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 2.1 — Market Structure
- Lessons: What is a market, bid-ask spread, order book, market makers and takers, price discovery
- Exercise: Order book reading exercise with live-like data
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 2.2 — Market Cycles
- Lessons: Bull and bear market phases, accumulation/distribution concepts, halving cycles and historical context
- Responsible Trading Insert: Historical cycles do not predict future cycles
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 2.3 — How to Research a Project
- Lessons: Whitepaper reading guide, tokenomics analysis, team evaluation, roadmap assessment, red flag checklist
- Exercise: Student evaluates a (historical) project from provided data pack
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions
- Format: Case study format

#### Module 2.4 — Liquidity and Volatility
- Lessons: What is liquidity, why thin markets are dangerous, volatility metrics, how news moves price
- Exercise: Scenario analysis — "A project with $2M daily volume vs $2B. What's the risk?"
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 2.5 — Portfolio Basics
- Lessons: Portfolio tracking tools, allocation principles, diversification concepts (and limits)
- Exercise: Build a simulated 5-asset portfolio and track it for one week
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

### Term 2 Assessments
- **Midterm:** Modules 2.1–2.3 (25 questions, 70% pass)
- **Final Exam:** All modules (45 questions, 70% pass)
- **Simulator Requirement:** Build and report on a simulated portfolio over 7 days
- **Project:** Write a 1-page research note on a major cryptocurrency using the provided framework
- **Certificate:** TecPey Academy Term 2 — Market Understanding

## Term 3 — Market Analysis (تحلیل بازار)

### Goal
Introduce technical and fundamental analysis as tools for informed decision-making — with explicit framing that analysis does not predict the future.

### Prerequisites
- Term 2 certificate or placement test score ≥ 80%

### Skills Acquired
1. Reading candlestick charts at an intermediate level
2. Applying common technical indicators with understanding of their limitations
3. Performing basic fundamental analysis on a crypto asset
4. Understanding sentiment analysis
5. Combining analysis methods without overconfidence

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 3.1 — Candlestick Charts
- Lessons: OHLC, candlestick patterns (15 most important), timeframes, reading trend
- Exercise: Identify patterns in 20 historical chart segments
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions
- Responsible Trading Insert: Patterns are probabilistic, not deterministic

#### Module 3.2 — Support & Resistance
- Lessons: S/R identification, trend lines, channels, key levels, why they work
- Exercise: Mark S/R on 5 historical charts, compare with model answers
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 3.3 — Technical Indicators
- Lessons: Moving averages (SMA, EMA), RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, volume indicators
- Exercise: Apply 3 indicators to a historical chart and identify a signal (not a prediction)
- Knowledge Check: 12 questions
- Responsible Trading Insert: Indicators lag price. No indicator predicts future price.

#### Module 3.4 — Fundamental Analysis
- Lessons: On-chain metrics basics, network activity, NVT ratio, developer activity, tokenomics red flags
- Exercise: Fundamental scorecard exercise on 3 historical projects
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 3.5 — Market Sentiment
- Lessons: Fear and Greed index, funding rates, open interest, social sentiment, media cycle
- Exercise: Sentiment analysis from historical data — "What was the market feeling in this moment?"
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 3.6 — Combining Analysis (Multi-Confluence)
- Lessons: Why single-indicator trading fails, multi-confluence decision framework, analysis hierarchy
- Exercise: Full analysis exercise on historical asset — technical + fundamental + sentiment
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

### Term 3 Assessments
- **Midterm:** Modules 3.1–3.3 (35 questions, 70% pass)
- **Final Exam:** All modules (50 questions, 70% pass)
- **Simulator Requirement:** 15 simulator trades with written rationale per trade
- **Assignment:** Full written analysis of one historical market event (chart + fundamental + sentiment)
- **Certificate:** TecPey Academy Term 3 — Market Analysis

## Term 4 — Risk Management (مدیریت ریسک)

### Goal
Build the foundational discipline that separates professional traders from gamblers. Risk management is treated as the most important term in the curriculum.

### Prerequisites
- Term 3 certificate or placement test score ≥ 80%

### Skills Acquired
1. Calculate position size relative to account risk
2. Place and manage stop-loss orders strategically
3. Define and maintain risk-reward ratio standards
4. Build a basic portfolio risk framework
5. Recognize when NOT to trade

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 4.1 — Why Traders Fail
- Lessons: Statistics on retail trader performance, common causes of failure, the role of risk management vs analysis
- Exercise: Post-mortem on 5 historical trading failures (patterns identified)
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 4.2 — Position Sizing
- Lessons: Fixed dollar, fixed percentage, Kelly Criterion (simplified), account size and risk tolerance
- Exercise: Calculator exercise — size 10 positions given different account sizes and risk parameters
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 4.3 — Stop-Loss Strategy
- Lessons: Technical stop placement, volatility-based stops (ATR), mental stop vs hard stop, stop hunting
- Exercise: Place stops on 10 historical chart scenarios
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 4.4 — Risk/Reward Ratio
- Lessons: Minimum acceptable R:R ratios, expectancy formula, why win rate alone is insufficient
- Exercise: Calculate expectancy for 5 strategy profiles
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 4.5 — Portfolio Risk
- Lessons: Correlation, concentration risk, drawdown metrics, maximum drawdown management, recovery math
- Exercise: Portfolio stress test on historical bear market data
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 4.6 — When Not to Trade
- Lessons: Low-quality setups, high-impact news events, low liquidity periods, personal state (tired, stressed, emotional)
- Exercise: Identify "no-trade" conditions in 10 scenarios
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

### Term 4 Assessments
- **Midterm:** Modules 4.1–4.3 (35 questions, 70% pass)
- **Final Exam:** All modules (50 questions, 70% pass)
- **Simulator Requirement:** 20 trades with risk parameters documented. AI review of risk compliance.
- **Project:** Personal Risk Management Plan (written, reviewed by AI Mentor)
- **Certificate:** TecPey Academy Term 4 — Risk Management

## Term 5 — Trading Psychology (روان‌شناسی معامله‌گری)

### Goal
Develop the psychological foundation of a disciplined market participant. This term is as important as risk management — most trading failures are psychological, not analytical.

### Prerequisites
- Term 4 certificate

### Skills Acquired
1. Identify personal psychological biases in trading
2. Recognize FOMO, revenge trading, overconfidence, loss aversion in real-time
3. Build a pre-trade and post-trade emotional ritual
4. Develop a personal trading rules document
5. Recover from losing streaks without destructive behavior

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 5.1 — The Psychology of Loss
- Lessons: Loss aversion research (Kahneman/Tversky), why losses feel twice as painful as gains, the disposition effect
- Exercise: Reflection journal — student recalls a financial or personal loss and analyzes their emotional response
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 5.2 — Cognitive Biases in Trading
- Lessons: Confirmation bias, anchoring, availability heuristic, gambler's fallacy, overconfidence
- Exercise: Identify biases in 10 trading scenario descriptions
- Knowledge Check: 12 questions

#### Module 5.3 — FOMO & Revenge Trading
- Lessons: FOMO mechanism (fear of missing out), revenge trading trigger and escalation pattern, how to interrupt the cycle
- Exercise: Role-play scenario — walk through a FOMO situation and practice pause technique
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 5.4 — The Trading Mindset
- Lessons: Process vs outcome orientation, probabilistic thinking, accepting uncertainty, patience as a skill
- Exercise: Mindset audit questionnaire with AI Mentor debrief
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 5.5 — Building Your Rulebook
- Lessons: What belongs in a trading rulebook, rule-setting principles, commitment mechanisms
- Exercise: Draft a personal trading rulebook (minimum 10 rules)
- AI Mentor Review: Reviews rulebook for completeness and internal consistency
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 5.6 — Recovery and Resilience
- Lessons: How to handle drawdowns, losing streaks, and the emotional aftermath of big losses
- Exercise: Recovery plan exercise — "You've lost 20% of your account this month. What do you do?"
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

### Term 5 Assessments
- **Midterm:** Modules 5.1–5.3 (30 questions, 70% pass)
- **Final Exam:** All modules (50 questions, 70% pass)
- **Simulator Requirement:** 30 trades over minimum 30 days with emotional log per trade
- **Behavioral Review:** AI Mentor generates Psychological Report based on simulator data
- **Certificate:** TecPey Academy Term 5 — Trading Psychology

## Term 6 — Strategy & System (استراتژی و سیستم)

### Goal
Build the capacity to design, test, and follow a personal trading system — with emphasis on rule-based execution over intuition.

### Prerequisites
- Term 5 certificate

### Skills Acquired
1. Define a complete trading strategy in writing
2. Backtest a strategy on historical data
3. Evaluate a strategy statistically (not just by P&L)
4. Build a trade journal discipline
5. Adapt a strategy based on performance data without emotional override

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 6.1 — What is a Trading System?
- Lessons: Components of a system (entry, exit, sizing, filters), why discretionary fails, rule-based vs hybrid
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 6.2 — Strategy Design
- Lessons: Entry conditions, exit conditions, position sizing rule, asset universe definition, timeframe selection
- Exercise: Design exercise — build a complete rule-based strategy
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 6.3 — Backtesting
- Lessons: What backtesting proves and doesn't prove, curve-fitting traps, walk-forward testing concept, sample size requirements
- Exercise: Manual backtest on provided historical data (minimum 50 trades)
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 6.4 — Performance Statistics
- Lessons: Win rate, expectancy, profit factor, max drawdown, Sharpe ratio concept, Calmar ratio
- Exercise: Calculate performance statistics for a provided trade log
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 6.5 — Trade Journal Mastery
- Lessons: What to record, how to review, pattern identification in journaled data, weekly review ritual
- Exercise: Build a 2-week simulator journal with AI Mentor review
- Knowledge Check: 8 questions

#### Module 6.6 — System Adaptation
- Lessons: When to adjust vs when to stay the course, deteriorating market conditions, the optimization trap
- Exercise: Case study — analyze a strategy that stopped working and determine cause
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

### Term 6 Assessments
- **Midterm:** Modules 6.1–6.3 (35 questions, 70% pass)
- **Final Exam:** All modules (50 questions, 70% pass)
- **Simulator Requirement:** 50 trades over 45 days following a documented strategy
- **Capstone Project:** Full trading system document + backtest results + 30-day simulator log
- **Certificate:** TecPey Academy Term 6 — Strategy & System

## Term 7 — Professionalization (حرفه‌ای‌سازی)

### Goal
Prepare the student for the realities of trading as a professional discipline — including career paths, continuous improvement culture, compliance, and community contribution.

### Prerequisites
- Term 6 certificate

### Skills Acquired
1. Understand career paths in crypto (trading, analysis, development, education, compliance)
2. Apply regulatory awareness to trading decisions
3. Design a continuous learning system
4. Contribute meaningfully to a trading community
5. Mentor junior students

### Module Breakdown

#### Module 7.1 — Career Paths in Crypto
- Lessons: Professional trader, proprietary trading, research analyst, DeFi developer, compliance, educator
- Exercise: Career alignment exercise with AI Mentor

#### Module 7.2 — Regulatory Awareness
- Lessons: Global regulatory landscape, Iran-specific regulations, AML/KYC as protection not surveillance, tax reporting principles
- Knowledge Check: 10 questions

#### Module 7.3 — Continuous Learning System
- Lessons: Learning from the market, building a reading system, community learning, mentorship dynamics
- Exercise: Design a personal 90-day development plan

#### Module 7.4 — Community Leadership
- Lessons: How to share knowledge without giving advice, building accountability partnerships, mentoring principles
- Exercise: Mentor a Term 1 student through their first week (supervised peer mentoring)

### Term 7 Assessments
- **Final Exam:** All modules (40 questions, 70% pass)
- **Capstone Review:** AI Mentor comprehensive review across all 7 terms
- **Graduation Ceremony:** Certificate of Graduation from TecPey Academy
- **Certificate:** TecPey Academy Graduate — 7-Term Completion
