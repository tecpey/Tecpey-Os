## Preamble

This document is the binding educational constitution for TecPey Academy. Every lesson, every assessment, every AI interaction, every certificate, and every piece of content produced for TecPey Academy must comply with the standards defined here.

This is not a style guide. It is a contract between TecPey and its students.

---

## Part 1 — Educational Philosophy

### 1.1 Core Belief

TecPey Academy exists because entering financial markets without education is dangerous.

Our educational philosophy rests on three pillars:

**1. Protect before enable.**
Before teaching a student how to trade, we teach them what can go wrong, how to identify risk, and how to develop the discipline to act within defined rules.

**2. Understand before memorize.**
Memorizing definitions without conceptual understanding produces exam-passers, not informed market participants. Every concept must be understood in context, not recited.

**3. Practice before live.**
No student graduates from any term without demonstrating the concept in a simulated environment. Knowledge without applied practice does not transfer to behavior.

### 1.2 What TecPey Academy Is Not

- A marketing channel
- A regulatory compliance document
- A funnel to the exchange
- A competitor to universities
- A replacement for professional financial advice

### 1.3 What TecPey Academy Is

- An independent educational institution within the TecPey ecosystem
- A structured, assessed, certified path from beginner to disciplined participant
- A responsible, science-backed, culturally authentic learning experience

## Part 2 — Learning Science Principles

All content design must be grounded in the following learning science principles:

### 2.1 Spaced Repetition (Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve)
- Key concepts must be revisited at expanding intervals: Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 21 → Day 60
- The platform must schedule review sessions automatically
- Flashcard system implements spaced repetition algorithm (SM-2 or equivalent)

### 2.2 Active Recall
- Students must retrieve information, not recognize it
- Every knowledge check uses free recall or application questions, not multiple-choice recognition unless explicitly justified
- Exercises require students to apply concepts to new scenarios

### 2.3 Interleaving
- Practice sessions must mix concepts from different modules, not just the current one
- Prevents illusion of competence from blocked practice

### 2.4 Desirable Difficulty
- Challenge level must be calibrated to be effortful but achievable
- Material that is too easy produces no learning. Material that is too hard produces dropout.
- AI Mentor must adjust difficulty based on demonstrated performance, not stated preference

### 2.5 Mastery-Gating
- Students cannot advance to the next lesson until they demonstrate competence in the current one
- Minimum passing threshold: 80% on all knowledge checks
- Failed attempts require full re-engagement with the material, not just re-taking the quiz

### 2.6 Elaborative Interrogation
- The AI Mentor uses "Why?" and "How?" questions to deepen understanding
- Never delivers answers. Guides students to derive them.

### 2.7 Concrete Examples Before Abstract Rules
- Every concept is taught through a concrete, relatable scenario first
- The abstract rule is stated after the example, not before
- Example must be drawn from Iranian market context wherever possible

### 2.8 Metacognition Prompts
- Students are regularly asked to assess their own understanding
- Confidence ratings after exercises are recorded and compared to actual performance
- Gap between confidence and performance is surfaced by the AI Mentor as a growth signal

## Part 3 — Content Standards

### 3.1 Writing Standards

**Language:** Native Persian for all primary content. English translation for /en path.

**Reading Level:** Persian content must be written at a reading level appropriate for an intelligent adult with no financial background. Assume intelligence, not prior knowledge.

**Sentence Length:** Maximum 25 words per sentence in explanations. Definitions may be longer.

**Technical Terms:** Every technical term is linked to the Glossary on first use in each lesson.

**Analogies:** Every complex concept must have at least one analogy drawn from everyday Iranian experience (bazaar, home savings, employment, etc.).

**Prohibited Content:**
- Price predictions
- Return projections
- Claims that any strategy "works" or "guarantees" outcomes
- Comparisons of specific assets as investment recommendations
- Copy of any competitor platform's explanations or exercises

### 3.2 Accuracy Standards

- All financial data cited must include date and source
- All statistics must link to primary sources
- Regulatory references must specify jurisdiction and version
- Content must be reviewed and updated within 90 days of any significant market event, regulatory change, or technology update that affects accuracy

### 3.3 Responsible Trading Statement

Every lesson in every module relating to trading, investment, or market participation must include, at minimum, one contextually placed responsible trading statement. This is not a disclaimer — it is integrated into the lesson as educational content.

**Approved Responsible Trading Statements (Persian):**

> این درس آموزشی است، نه توصیه مالی. هیچ استراتژی‌ای در بازار سود تضمین‌شده ندارد.

> مدیریت ریسک اولین مهارت یک معامله‌گر حرفه‌ای است، نه آخرین.

> قبل از هر معامله‌ای با سرمایه واقعی، آن را در محیط شبیه‌سازی تمرین کنید.

**English equivalents are provided in the /en lesson path.**

## Part 4 — Practical Standards

### 4.1 Simulation Requirement
Every module that introduces a trading or analytical concept must include a linked simulator exercise before the module assessment can be unlocked.

### 4.2 Exercise Design
- Exercises must present a scenario the student has not seen before
- The scenario must require applying the concept in a new context
- Exercises are not repeatable with the same scenario (scenario pool per module, minimum 10 per assessment point)

### 4.3 Real Data Integration
- Market examples in lessons use real historical data where possible
- Data must be labeled as historical and include the date
- Never use "example prices" that could be mistaken for current market data

### 4.4 Skill Demonstration Standard
For any module at Level 3 or above, theoretical knowledge is insufficient. Students must demonstrate the skill in the Trading Arena simulator before certification is awarded.

## Part 5 — Psychology Standards

### 5.1 Emotional Literacy
TecPey Academy teaches trading psychology as a core competency, not an elective. Every term includes explicit psychological training.

**Required psychological concepts per term:**
- Loss aversion and how it distorts decision-making
- Overconfidence effect in beginners
- FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) identification and mitigation
- Revenge trading recognition and prevention
- Confirmation bias in analysis
- Anchoring to price entry points
- Sunk cost fallacy in position management

### 5.2 Emotional State in Practice
The Trading Arena simulator includes an emotional state log per trade. Students are required to:
1. Record their emotional state before entering a position
2. Record the reason for the trade
3. Record their emotional state at exit
4. Review their emotional pattern in the weekly AI Mentor session

### 5.3 Psychological Safety
- The platform must never shame students for wrong answers
- Error messages are framed as learning signals, not failures
- AI Mentor responses use encouraging, non-judgmental language
- Streak-break messaging must not produce anxiety — it must invite return

### 5.4 Behavioral Intervention
If the AI Mentor detects a pattern of revenge trading, FOMO behavior, or rule violations exceeding a defined threshold, it must:
1. Pause the simulator session
2. Present a reflection prompt
3. Require completion of a behavioral module before resuming

## Part 6 — Ethics Standards

### 6.1 No Financial Advice
TecPey Academy is an educational institution. It does not provide investment advice, trading recommendations, or return projections under any circumstances.

The AI Mentor must refuse any request for:
- Specific asset purchase/sell recommendations
- Price predictions
- Strategy endorsement for live trading

### 6.2 No Conflict of Interest
Academy content must not prioritize TecPey-listed assets or TecPey products over educational objectives. Content that mentions TecPey exchange features must clearly distinguish educational context from promotional context.

### 6.3 Disclosure Standards
- AI-generated content must be labeled
- Human-reviewed content must be labeled with reviewer credentials
- Sponsorship or partnership content must be clearly disclosed and is only permitted if it does not influence educational content

### 6.4 Data Ethics
- Student performance data is used only to personalize learning
- Student data is never used for marketing profiling
- Students can export or delete their Academy data at any time
- AI Mentor conversations are not used to train external AI models without explicit, informed consent

## Part 7 — Assessment Rules

### 7.1 Quiz Standards
- Minimum 5 questions per knowledge check
- Minimum 10 questions per module quiz
- Minimum 30 questions per term exam
- All questions reviewed by at least two subject matter reviewers before publication
- Question bank must have minimum 3x coverage (30 questions per 10-question quiz) to prevent memorization of specific questions

### 7.2 Passing Standards
| Assessment Type | Minimum Pass | Retake Policy |
|----------------|-------------|---------------|
| Knowledge Check | 80% | Immediate, after reviewing material |
| Module Quiz | 75% | After 24-hour cooldown, material review required |
| Term Midterm | 70% | After 48-hour cooldown, instructor review prompt |
| Term Final Exam | 70% | After 72-hour cooldown, AI Mentor session required |

### 7.3 Anti-Cheating Standards
- Exam questions are randomized from pool
- Timer-based assessments at Term level and above
- Answer pattern analysis (too-fast responses flagged for manual review)
- Final Exams require simulator activity in the preceding 7 days to be valid

### 7.4 Grading Philosophy
Assessments are not gotcha tests. They are diagnostic tools. Every wrong answer produces a targeted explanation and a recommendation for further study.

## Part 8 — Certification Rules

### 8.1 Certificate Issuance Conditions
A TecPey Academy certificate is issued only when the student has:
1. Completed all required lessons in the term
2. Passed all module quizzes at or above minimum threshold
3. Passed the final term exam at or above minimum threshold
4. Completed the required simulator exercises for that term
5. Maintained a minimum study consistency score (no gap longer than 21 days in active terms)

### 8.2 Certificate Content
Each certificate must contain:
- Student full name
- Term name and number
- Completion date
- Unique certificate ID
- QR code linking to live verification page
- Score (%)
- TecPey Academy seal
- Responsible Trading Statement

### 8.3 Certificate Validity
- Certificates are permanently verifiable on `tecpey.ir/verify/[certificateId]`
- Certificates are not revoked unless evidence of fraud is confirmed
- Market regulation changes may prompt TecPey to add version notes to older certificates

### 8.4 Certificate Fraud Prevention
- Certificate IDs are cryptographically signed
- Duplicate-issue prevention at database level
- Manual review process for certificates generated in less than the minimum expected study time

## Part 9 — Localization Rules

### 9.1 Persian-First
Persian (fa-IR) is the primary language of TecPey Academy. All content originates in Persian and is adapted (not translated) for English.

### 9.2 Cultural Adaptation
- Examples use Iranian market context: Toman, Iran-based price scenarios, common Iranian behavioral patterns with money
- Do not use examples from US or European financial culture that have no resonance in Iran
- Islamic finance concepts must be addressed where relevant (interest, riba, halal investment discussion)

### 9.3 Financial Vocabulary
- Establish a TecPey standard Persian financial glossary
- All technical terms are standardized across the platform
- Do not invent new Persian terms for concepts that have accepted Persian equivalents

### 9.4 RTL Design
- All Persian lessons are rendered in RTL
- Mathematical formulas use standard notation with RTL label formatting
- Charts and diagrams must be readable in RTL context

## Part 10 — Translation Rules

### 10.1 English Path (/en)
English content is a cultural adaptation of Persian content, not a word-for-word translation.

Adaptation process:
1. Persian lesson is written and reviewed
2. Translator/adaptor produces English version with English-appropriate examples
3. Technical review for accuracy
4. Native English speaker review for clarity
5. Publication

### 10.2 Translation Prohibited Practices
- Machine translation without human review
- Literal translation of Persian idioms
- Using English content from competitor platforms as reference
- Skipping context-setting that is necessary for English speakers unfamiliar with Iranian market context

## Part 11 — Accessibility Rules

### 11.1 WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance
All Academy pages must meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards.

### 11.2 Screen Reader Support
- All images have descriptive alt text
- All charts have text equivalents
- Video captions are available for all lesson videos
- Interactive exercises have keyboard navigation

### 11.3 Cognitive Accessibility
- Maximum paragraph length: 4 sentences
- Progress indicators on all exercises and assessments
- Clear visual hierarchy in all lesson pages
- Consistent navigation pattern across all lessons

### 11.4 Low-Bandwidth Support
- Lesson text must be accessible on 2G connections
- Video is optional, not required for lesson completion
- Critical content must not be embedded only in images or video

## Part 12 — Mobile Learning Rules

### 12.1 Mobile-First Lesson Design
All lessons must be designed for 390px viewport first. Desktop is an enhancement.

### 12.2 Micro-Session Design
Each lesson module must be completable in 10–15 minutes on mobile. Longer content must be segmented.

### 12.3 Offline Support
- Lesson text and images must be cacheable for offline reading
- Quiz answers can be submitted when connectivity is restored
- Offline state must be communicated clearly

### 12.4 Mobile Interaction
- Tap targets minimum 44×44 px
- No hover-only interactions
- Flashcard swipe gestures supported

## Part 13 — AI Mentor Rules

### 13.1 Scope of AI Mentor
The AI Mentor is an educational assistant. It is not a financial advisor, not a trading coach, and not a support agent.

**The AI Mentor may:**
- Explain concepts from the curriculum
- Ask Socratic questions to deepen understanding
- Identify weak areas from performance data
- Suggest next steps in the learning path
- Encourage and motivate
- Analyze simulator performance for behavioral patterns

**The AI Mentor must never:**
- Recommend specific assets to buy or sell
- Predict price movements
- Comment on current market conditions as investment signal
- Provide information outside the educational scope
- Claim to have information it does not have

### 13.2 Refusal Language
When the AI Mentor receives an out-of-scope request:
> "این سوال خارج از محدوده آموزشی من است. من می‌توانم در یادگیری مفاهیم و تمرین مهارت‌ها کمکت کنم، اما توصیه‌ای درباره معاملات واقعی نمی‌دهم."

### 13.3 AI Content Labeling
All AI-generated explanations must include a disclosure label visible in the UI.

## Part 15 — Content Review Process

### 15.1 Lesson Authorship → Review → Publication Flow

```
Draft (Author)
    ↓
Technical Review (Subject Matter Expert)
    ↓
Educational Review (Learning Designer)
    ↓
Responsible Trading Review (Ethics Check)
    ↓
Persian Language Review (Linguistic Quality)
    ↓
UI/UX Integration (Design Review)
    ↓
QA (Rendering, links, quiz validation)
    ↓
Publication
```

### 15.2 Review Turnaround Standards
- Technical Review: 5 business days
- Educational Review: 3 business days
- Responsible Trading Review: 2 business days
- Language Review: 3 business days
- Total maximum pipeline: 15 business days

### 15.3 Emergency Update Protocol
If a lesson contains factually incorrect information discovered post-publication:
1. Lesson is flagged as "Under Review" within 24 hours
2. Students mid-lesson are notified
3. Corrected version is published within 72 hours
4. Version change is logged in lesson history

## Part 16 — Curriculum Governance

### 16.1 Curriculum Committee
The Academy Curriculum Committee is responsible for all curricular decisions. It consists of:
- Academy Director (final approval authority)
- Learning Designer (educational architecture)
- Subject Matter Expert (crypto/finance accuracy)
- Student Representative (learner perspective)
- AI Systems Lead (AI Mentor integration)

### 16.2 Curriculum Change Process
- Any modification to level structure, passing thresholds, or certification requirements requires committee approval
- Changes affecting existing students must include a migration plan
- Changes are versioned (curriculum v1.0, v1.1, etc.)

### 16.3 Versioning
- Major curriculum version (v1.0 → v2.0): Complete restructuring of levels or learning objectives
- Minor curriculum version (v1.0 → v1.1): Module additions, removal of outdated content, assessment updates
- Patch version (v1.0.0 → v1.0.1): Content corrections, question updates, typo fixes
